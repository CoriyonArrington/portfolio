Summary

Insights

In the aftermath of George Floyd, people have wanted to support black-owned businesses. However, there are not many apps or tools that help connect people to black-owned businesses, products, or services. There are a few problems that exist though.

Problems

  1. There are tons of black businesses that are hard to find.
  2. If a black business is found, there is no way to verify that it is actually black-owned.
  3. The apps that are available are geared mostly towards restaurants and listings are not regularly updated.
  4. There is not a place or way to easily recommend black businesses.

Assumptions

  1. People want to support black businesses
  2. They want to share resources and information easily.
  3. Some audiences feel alienated.

Solutions

  1. Create a directory that is available for every type of business available.
  2. Showcase a way to show that the business is legitimately black-owned.
  3. Provide a way for the users to recommend black businesses be added.

Discovery & Research

User Survey

I decided to start out the process by creating a user survey on Google Forms because I wanted to test my assumptions and see if people were having an experience similar to mine. When George Floyd was killed, I found myself wanting to support black businesses even more than usual. There were tons of "check out these" lists available but finding a black business nearby was difficult to say the least. So, the user survey helped me to get insight.

Survey Highlights

Out of 65 participants

95% of people would use a tool designed primarily for finding black-owned businesses.

70% of those who searched for a black business before said they search at least two times a month.

57% said that finding black businesses was very hard or somewhat difficult.

Searching by business type was the top requested feature.

Incorrect search results ranked as a top pain point.

This showed me that there was a need for people to find black-owned businesses and there is an opportunity to improve the experience.

Competitive Analysis

The competitive analysis was crucial to the process. My main goals and reasons for doing the analysis were:

  1. Identify all major competitors
  2. Discover pros and cons for each
  3. Find areas to distinguish FindBlack
  4. Understand marketing, branding, and positioning

To my surprise, there were a lot more out there than I was expecting. Even as I continued throughout the project I would find more. I noticed that most of them were for finding restaurants though. Even still, the ones that were centered around finding any type of business were usually not designed very well or had many bugs. I was able to narrow down my competitors to the three below.

Information Architecture

User Stories and Flows

Now that it's been confirmed that there is a need and the audience has been determined it's a good time to create user stories to highlight the core features of the product.

  1. As a new user I want to quickly view various business categories.
  2. As a new user I want to search for businesses by keywords.
  3. As a new user I want to view pictures and business information.
  4. As a returning user I want to see new business highlights.
  5. As a returning user I want to view favorite businesses.
  6. As a returning user I want to see recommend a business get added.

The user stories were then converted into user flows using Creately to illustrate how the user would interact with the product and accomplish those goals.

Digital Wireframes

Once the user flows were complete, it was time to hit the drawing board. The survey results showed me which features people would like to see. Additionally, and most importantly, it showed me which ones they would use most often. So, I decided to start working on sketches with those priorities in mind.

People mentioned that they would use a search feature the most. So, I focused on ease-of-use with the search functionality, showcasing it early on in the new user flow and at the top of every screen. Secondly, I made sure there were several categories for them to choose to search from as well. These choices can be seen in the design below.

Lastly, I took a note from the competitors shown earlier and used a tab bar that had the core features: a home screen, search button, favorite option, and menu for them to do everything else.

Visual Design

Brand Characteristics

FindBlack was inspired by George Floyd and the different shades of black people. The goal is to have the users perceive the product as inclusive and easy to use.

Color

Sinopia #C04F27 Cultured #F5F5F5, the top two colors were chosen for FindBlack. Sinopia is a rich brown-red color that evokes a sense of strength and reliability. Given how users perceive other products as not having enough information or being reliable this was important. Cultured is a term used in the black community that means well refined and tasteful. It's also an off-white that conveys simplicity. Lastly, it complements Sinopia well. For these reasons, it was chosen as the second main color of FindBlack.

Logo Design

The logo was designed with the well-known resist fist. It's a staple and was used because it stands out.

Iterations

FindBlack went through a multiple rounds of user testing. The user testing led the design choices. Below are a few examples showcasing the iterations it went through and changes that were made.

Going into the project, I wanted to put an emphasis on usability and one of the choices I made was putting a skip button early on in the sign up flow. This allows the user to bypass the onboarding and immediately experience the app. However, it was brought to my attention that the skip button was heard to see. So, I redesigned it included it near the join and sign in button instead. Lastly, I changed it from "skip" to "browse as guest" to make the action more clear.

In the example above, I received tons of great feedback about the business page.

  • Red - It was mentioned that there were too many "cards" and the business page was too long. Coupled with this was the ability to get to the contact information sooner. So, I redesigned to remove the contact card and instead put the contact icons at the top of the page, changing its importance and saving space.
  • Yellow - User feedback showed that there was too much information going on in the initial business info section. To change this, I added the information to the photo that was showcased and used Auto Layout so that if more icons needed to be added it could easily be done while maintaining the spacing.
  • Purple - The about section was important because users wanted to see the business was legitimately black-owned. One easy way to accomplish this is to showcase the about section and show the owners. Feedback showed that the read more was hard to see and users were not sure how to expand it. So, I changed it nu adding an icon instead of the ellipses and it was much more clear.

The Conclusion

There are still aspects I plan to continue to improve and refine with more time. For example, I received some recent user feedback from a Product Designer that my home page should be more of a search screen. Initial user feedback did not showcase this but it's an interesting point from a product perspective. I plan on continuing further iterations with this in mind.

What I learned

One of the most challenging parts of the project was making designs that were flexible. For example, I wanted to make sure that whatever device it was viewed on that it would look as intended. I solved this by learning how to use constraints and auto layout effectively. Doing so helped me to create more responsive components which led to better overall design and structure.
